AURORA BOREALIS

The Solar Wind

The Sun releases charged particles — electrons and protons — known as the Solar Wind. During solar flares, this intensifies dramatically.

Earth's Magnetic Shield

The Magnetosphere deflects solar wind, but at the poles it funnels particles into the upper atmosphere.

Atomic Collisions

Solar particles smash into Oxygen and Nitrogen (100–300 km up). Excited atoms release energy as photons.

Aurora Colors

Oxygen ≤250km: Green/yellow

Oxygen >250km: Deep red

Nitrogen: Blue/purple/pink

💡 AI Insight

Auroras occur on other planets! Jupiter has the most powerful in the solar system. Saturn and Uranus also have their own light shows.
