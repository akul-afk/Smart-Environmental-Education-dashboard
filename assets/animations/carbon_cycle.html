THE CARBON CYCLE

The Biosphere (Life)

Carbon is life's building block. Through Photosynthesis, plants pull CO₂ from the atmosphere, using sunlight to lock it into solid biomass.

Through Respiration, animals and plants consume organic carbon for energy, exhaling CO₂ back into the atmosphere.

The Hydrosphere (Ocean)

The oceans are the largest active carbon sink. Through Air-Sea Exchange, CO₂ dissolves into cold waters and outgasses in warmer regions.

Marine Snow: Dead phytoplankton slowly drift into the abyss. This "biological pump" traps carbon in the deep ocean for thousands of years.

The Lithosphere (Earth)

Dead matter buried before decay gets compressed over millions of years into Fossil Fuels — coal, oil, and gas. This safely removes carbon from the active cycle.

The Human Impact

By mining and burning fossil fuels (Combustion), we release carbon that took millions of years to store in just decades. This overwhelms natural sinks, wrapping Earth in a thickening thermal blanket and driving climate change.

Atmospheric CO₂ has risen from 280 ppm (pre-industrial) to over 424 ppm in 2024 — a 50% increase. The ocean has absorbed roughly 30% of human-emitted CO₂, but at the cost of a 26% increase in acidity.
