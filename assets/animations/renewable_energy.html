CLEAN POWER LAB

Wind Energy

Kinetic to Electric: Aerodynamic blades catch wind, spinning a rotor connected to a massive generator in the nacelle. Spinning magnets induce a flow of electrons in copper coils.

Hydroelectric Power

Gravity & Water: A dam holds back water, creating a reservoir. Gravity forces water down through a penstock, driving a heavy water turbine (like a Francis turbine) linked to a generator.

Solar PV Systems

The Photovoltaic Effect: Sunlight (photons) strikes silicon layers, knocking electrons loose. An electric field forces them into a circuit, creating Direct Current (DC). An inverter then converts it to grid-ready Alternating Current (AC).

The Smart Grid

Generated power goes to a Substation Transformer which drastically increases the voltage. High voltage is pushed through Transmission Lines over vast distances with minimal energy loss before being stepped down for city consumption.

💡 AI Insight

Hydropower currently accounts for over 50% of global renewable generation. As wind and solar expand, hydro dams will act as massive 'water batteries,' modifying flow rates to fill gaps when the sun isn't shining or wind isn't blowing.
